Bedford-Stuyvesant

Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn and the blocks surrounding Von King Park, have a long history. The park, which was originally named for the convergence of Bedford, a settlement, and Stuyvesant, named for New York City's last Dutch Governor.

Von King Park was one of the first public parks in Brooklyn. It was originally called Tompkins Park, and was designed in part by Frederick Law Olmsted, who designed Prospect Park and Central Park.

Painter William Merritt Chase depicts it here in 1887.

An 1898 map of the area. You can see Von King was at this time still called Tompkins Park.

The opening of direct subway transit between Manhattan and Bed-Stuy in the '30s opened the neighborhood to African-American migration. This is an image from around that time.

Handball is still as popular now in Von King Park as it was in the 60s and 70s.
